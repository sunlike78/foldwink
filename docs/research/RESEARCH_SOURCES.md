# Research Sources — Foldwink Content Expansion

This log records the web research used to inspire Foldwink puzzle categories. **No puzzle boards, lists, or wording were copied from these sources.** They were consulted for general domain vocabulary (common clouds, common moons, common pasta shapes, etc.) which is public knowledge; original Foldwink puzzles were then authored by hand from that inspiration and validated locally.

## Research principles

- **Inspiration only.** Never store copied puzzle boards, never reuse puzzle wording.
- **General knowledge.** Prefer Wikipedia, NOAA, NASA, Britannica, museum and educational-domain pages — sources that document publicly-known domain taxonomies.
- **Multiple sources per theme.** Cross-check to avoid single-source bias.
- **No puzzle-site visits.** No queries or visits to NYT Connections, Wordle variants, or competing grouping games.
- **Public-knowledge filter.** "Types of clouds" is public knowledge. "Yesterday's Connections solution" is not.

## What "inspiration only" means in practice

Example (pasta shapes):

- **Researched:** Wikipedia's `List of pasta` confirms that penne, fusilli, rigatoni, and farfalle are all well-known dried short pasta shapes. Common knowledge, publicly documented.
- **Authored by hand for Foldwink:** a new puzzle "Boxed pantry" where the _Short Pasta_ group is authored as `["Penne", "Fusilli", "Rigatoni", "Farfalle"]`. No wording, no board layout, no false-trail design was borrowed from any source — only the fact that these shapes exist and belong to one domain.
- **Never written down:** any ready-made 16-card puzzle board or Foldwink-ready category set from any third party.

## Research gaps flagged

- Film genres vs. subgenres overlap is tricky (comedy can be TV, film, or book) — used only to confirm that genre-labels exist, not as a source of puzzles.
- Ocean/reef animals query returned mostly marketing content — did not use.
- "Dog breeds as marine animals" metaphor avoided as too gimmicky.

## Not consulted (and why)

- NYT / Connections site: off-limits (competing format).
- Puzzle-puzzle answer sites: off-limits.
- Any daily-puzzle archive: off-limits.
- Reddit puzzle communities: avoided to prevent accidental leak of recent puzzle content into drafts.
